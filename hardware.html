Hardware Summary

The final design is centered on the Quanser QArm, a 4 degree-of-freedom articulated manipulator with a gripper and RGB-D camera. This hardware stack let the team combine grasping, board observation, and controlled placement in a single compact platform. The hardware choices mattered because the system needed repeatable grasping while tokens appeared at multiple locations across the stage and board.

QArm platform

The QArm is modeled as a roll-pitch-pitch-roll serial manipulator with joints for base, shoulder, elbow, and wrist motion. In this application, the first three joints mainly determine position while the wrist is used mostly to orient the gripper at the target. That split made it easier to treat pickup and placement as mostly position-planning tasks.

Workspace and deadzone

The workspace defines where the end effector can reach and operate, while the deadzone marks areas where the arm may approach singular behavior. These limits shaped how the stage and board were arranged and influenced the path-planning choices used during game moves.

Manipulator schematic used to support the symbolic DH parameter model.

Kinematics

Forward kinematics were described with a standard Denavit-Hartenberg formulation to map joint angles to end-effector pose. Inverse kinematics were derived geometrically by solving the base angle from the horizontal projection and then reducing the remaining problem to a planar two-link system in the radial-vertical plane.

Arm motion approach

The arm motion was defined partly through Quanser inverse kinematics functions and partly through stored joint-angle positions. This worked well because both staging coordinates and board-cell coordinates could be defined numerically and saved in dictionaries for reuse.

Interference and intermediate waypoints

The main hardware problem during gameplay was avoiding collisions with tokens already placed on the board or still waiting in the stage. To prevent that, the team manually taught intermediate positions and then recreated those safe poses through saved joint angles. That added extra path planning without changing the overall move logic.
